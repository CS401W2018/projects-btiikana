Germany Adventure Awaits

I had a German friend back in high school that was very smart and funny. He was the only German guy in class and he was different but everyday he would always bring Pretzel snacks to school and would always tell everyone that it's a common snack in Germany. He would write the longest German words on the whiteboard such as "Rinderkennzeichnungsfleischetikettierungsüberwachungsaufgabenübertragungsgesetz" just to be funny and I doubted the word was even a word but he wasn't lying. I later found out that Pretzels were originated from the part of Europe that is now Germany and they were also enjoyed back in the middle ages around the 610 A.D. Additionally, the word "Rinderkennzeichnungsfleischetikettierungsüberwachungsaufgabenübertragungsgesetz" was in fact a German word that has something to do with managing and labeling the cow meats or beef which is super funny and interesting.

It's interesting to me how they could just come up with the word "Rinderkennzeichnungsfleischetikettierungsüberwachungsaufgabenübertragungsgesetz" and why, if you ever wonder! I mean we are unaware of the purpose, reasons, and history of most of the things that we see everyday, and the things that exist around us. For instance, "Pretzels", I buy and enjoy Pretzels every once in a while but I never knew that it's originated from Germany. I really like that fact that Germany has more for the eyes to see and for the mind to learn pertaining to culture, other than what's mentioned. I would love to know more about the other things that make Germany super unique and interesting, perhaps not now but soon.
