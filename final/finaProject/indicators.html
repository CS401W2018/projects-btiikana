Indicators

Charts

A Chart is like your eye visions inside the trading world. In order to see what is happening in the markets you have to have Charts. A chart is a canvas that can be altered to one's preference and it serves to display traditional and powerful Indicators to gain insights of the price movements in the market.

What I use:

  • Regular Chart - a canvas used to visually mark and see the movement of prices.
  • Time and Sales - a digital table with columns and rows to display the amount of recent transactions from buyers and sellers for a given valuable (instrument) in the market.
Regular Chart
  • Unlimited Customization
  • Canvas for Indicators
  • It's easier to use.
Time and Sales
  • Limited Customization.
  • Table Format.
  • You have to read numbers fast.

Strategy

What makes up a good trading strategy is having the right tools that work for you. Aside from choosing the best platforms out there with chartless features or automated order execution softwares, most of the time you will always end up having to use simple and easy to use Indicators. These indicators are usually displayed on a chart and they require your manual input, focused-attention and quick judgment. While there are numerous Indicators available, I'll focus on sharing the ones I find simple and useful for me.

What I use:

  • Average True Range (ATR) - A projection for the price movement of an instrument in a day.
  • Fair Value Gaps (FVG) - Gaps that represent unfilled orders in the orderbook.
  • Volume - Indicates the busiest hours and the amount of orders being executed at large numbers.
  • 21 Exponential Moving Average - A tool useful to inform traders of the average price for the instrument for the recent 21 periods.

How I use these Indicators

ATR - I mark possible limit areas where the prices are likely to stop that way I know where to focus on.

Volume - I watch this indicator carefully to make a decision whether to participate or not. You have to be super fast when the volume is high but I prefer low volume atmosphere to scope out possible entries.

Fair Value Gap - I use this to start looking for opportunities to enter or put on a trade because I know these gaps will pull prices towards them since they need to be filled.

21 EMA - I carefully watch this line, if the prices cross this line to the upside then I will buy and when the prices are crossing this line (green line shown on 21 EMA image)

How I exit a Trade:

1. When I hit my Target.
2. When the prices are going against me. I can tolerate a 10% loss.
3. When I don't understand the patterns or movement of the market. Most of the time the market is too tricky.

Execution

After finding a preferred platform and the right strategy to make informed decisions you will then need to put on a trade. Below, I will mention what tools I use only to execute my buy or sell order in the market.

What I use:

  • One Click Trading - A built in tool to quickly execute orders with one click.
  • Depth of Market - A tool that will allow you to execute your orders and display your orders on the BID and ASK table.
