Platforms

Trading platforms are places where you can find trading products to trade. Simply, these platforms can be either Exchange entities or Trading Provider companies, also some host services on mobile devices (I don't use) but most are on desktop.
Below are brief explanation of what these platforms are and a list of trading platforms that I normally use but this depends on their fluctuating commission rates.

Exhchange Entity - This is an organization that makes and sells the investment products.

Trading Provider/ Brokerage - This is a company that acts like a middle man to allow individual retail traders like us to have access to products that the exchange entity sells. This entity runs promotions and makes money from commissions.

What I use:
  • Ninja Trader - Trading Provider
  • Tradovate - Trading Provider
  • Tradestation - Trading Provider
  • CME Groups - Exchange Entity, where I buy my preferred Futures Products from but through a provider.

Although Exchange Entities have their own brokerage services, I have never used them as I prefer Trading Providers personally for their charting features and intergration. Below are the pictures of the platforms I use as listed above and the tools they offer.
